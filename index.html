About Me

I am a cognitive neuroscientist with an interest in mental health science. I completed my PhD at the University of Cambridge, where I spent four years researching the effects of stress and trauma on cognition. Currently, as a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Melbourne, my work focuses on developing a new digital mental health assessment tool for young people.

I collaborate closely with Clinical Psychologists and individuals with lived experience to ensure that our digital mental health tool is both clinically meaningful and practically useful. My broader research interests include harnessing digital technologies to improve mental health outcomes and expanding access to mental health services.
